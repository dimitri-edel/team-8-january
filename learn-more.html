Learn More About Modern Slavery

Understand the global issue of modern slavery and how you can take action.

What is Modern Slavery?

Modern slavery refers to situations where people are forced, coerced, or deceived into working against their will. Victims often live in fear, unable to leave or escape their situation due to threats or manipulation.

  • Human trafficking
  • Forced labor
  • Child labor
  • Domestic servitude
  • Sexual exploitation

In the UK, the Modern Slavery Act 2015 outlines legal protections against forced labor, servitude, and trafficking.

Key UK Statistics:

  • An estimated 136,000 people in the UK are living in modern slavery (Global Slavery Index, 2018).
  • Over 10,000 potential victims were referred to the National Referral Mechanism (NRM) in 2019 (UK Home Office).
  • Sex trafficking and labor exploitation are the most common forms in the UK.

Key Facts About Modern Slavery

  • 40 million people globally are victims of modern slavery, with 10 million of them being children (ILO, 2021).
  • In the UK, 70% rise in modern slavery offenses since the Modern Slavery Act 2015 (UK Home Office).
  • The Modern Slavery Helpline receives hundreds of calls each month.
  • Criminal networks involved in modern slavery generate billions annually.

The Different Forms of Modern Slavery

1. Human Trafficking:

People are illegally moved for forced labor or sexual exploitation.

2. Forced Labor:

Individuals work under threats of violence or abuse. Common industries: agriculture, construction, and manufacturing.

3. Domestic Servitude:

Victims work in private homes under abusive conditions, often hidden from public view.

4. Sexual Exploitation:

Victims are forced into sexual activity for the benefit of traffickers.

5. Child Slavery:

Children are exploited for labor or sexual purposes, or involved in illegal activities.

6. Organ Trafficking:

People are coerced into giving up organs for transplantation.

How You Can Make a Difference

1. Educate Yourself and Others

Learn to recognize the signs of modern slavery and spread awareness.

2. Support Modern Slavery Charities

Donate or volunteer to organizations that help survivors of modern slavery. Some key charities include:

  • Unseen UK - Provides support to victims and works on preventing human trafficking.
  • Hope for Justice - Offers services such as victim support and awareness campaigns.
  • Anti-Slavery International - Works on advocacy and policy change to end slavery.

3. Report Suspected Cases of Modern Slavery

If you suspect that someone is a victim of modern slavery, report it to the authorities. In the UK, you can contact:

  • The Modern Slavery Helpline: Call 08000 121 700
  • The National Crime Agency (NCA): Visit NCA Modern Slavery Unit
  • Crimestoppers UK: Report to Crimestoppers - Modern Slavery

4. Lobby for Stronger Legislation

Support campaigns that demand tougher penalties for traffickers and better protections for survivors.

5. Be a Responsible Consumer

Be mindful of where the goods you purchase come from and look for certifications like Fairtrade and Ethical Trading Initiative (ETI).

6. Volunteer and Support Survivor Rehabilitation

There are organizations that provide rehabilitation services for survivors of modern slavery. Volunteering with them can help offer support and healing to those who have suffered.
